Embrace Nature Symphony

Weather, in all its variability, is a complex puzzle created by nature. Analysing the weather patterns is crucial towards mitigating the impacts of climate change and fostering a more sustainable future. The data in this visualisation reflects the daily weather information for capital cities around the world from 29 August 2023 to 13 September 2023.

Exploring Regions of Droughts and Deluge

Too Dry

Regions like Saudi Arabia, Iraq and Namibia are characterized by arid deserts and minimal rainfall.

Too Wet

Tropical climate zones like Africa, South America, Philippines, and Micronesia have lush rainforests and swampy areas.

Continental Wind Speed Variations

Australia: High Wind Speed (North West)

During August and September, the Australian monsoon starts retreating. This may result in increased northwest winds as the monsoonal rains diminish.

Atlantic: High Wind Speed (South East)

High wind speeds in the southeast direction in the Atlantic Ocean can be influenced by the movement of tropical cyclones (hurricanes or typhoons).

Forest Fires

In regions with a history of wildfires, such as Indonesia, Chile, and parts of China , the burning of forests contribute to high CO levels, especially during dry seasons.

Industrialisation

Rapid industrialization in countries like China and parts of Indonesia increases emissions of carbon monoxide from factories and vehicles.

Biomass Burning

In agricultural regions like Indonesia, the use of biomass burning for land clearing and crop management can release carbon monoxide into the air.

Unveiling the Ozone Hotspots

Ozone is a vital component of the Earth's atmosphere. It shields us from harmful solar radiation, creating the ozone layer. However, when it's found at ground level, it becomes a major air pollutant.
